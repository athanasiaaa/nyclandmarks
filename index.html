The city of New York is home to many famous iconic landmarks. Some of these historic landmarks include: Times Square, the Empire State building, The Statue of Liberty, and The Freedom Tower. These grand tourist attractions have captured the spirit of the city over the years. The state of New York has definitely earned it's nickname 'the big apple' due to its fast-paced development in fashion, the arts, culture, global influence, and finance. Breathtaking views from Top of the Rock, the Empire State Building and One World Observatory. Exhibits from throughout history at the Metropolitan Museum of Art, the Brooklyn Museum and New York Hall of Science. Thrilling performances at Lincoln Center, BAM and Barclays Center. Attractions in NYC suit all tastes. It is this charismatic atmosphere that personifies the city of New York and lures the rest of the world like a magnet to the big apple. Today, more than 36,000 buildings throughout New York City are protected as individual landmarks, interior landmarks, scenic landmarks, or as part of 141 historic districts. According to the Landmarks Law, one of the purposes of safeguarding the buildings and places that represent New York City's history is to promote the use of designated landmarks for the education, pleasure and welfare of the people of the City. On this site, you will find some of these landmarks that truly capture the spirit of the big apple.
